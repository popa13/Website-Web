About me

My name is Pierre-Olivier Parisé. Since Fall 2024, I am an assistant professor (tenure track position) at the Université of Quebec in Three Rivers.

Previously, I was a temporary assistant professor at the Department of Mathematics of the University of Hawaii at Manoa, working alongside Malik Younsi. My research was supported by a postdoctoral research schorlarship (B3X) and an NSERC postdoctoral scholarship.

I received my Ph. D. from Laval University (QC, Ca). My supervisor was Thomas J. Ransford. My research was funded by the Alexander-Graham Bell Graduate scholarship from NSERC and the graduate scholarship from the FRQNT.

I obtained a Masters' degree in 2017 from Université du Québec à Trois-Rivières. My supervisor was Dominic Rochon. My research was funded by the Alexander-Graham Bell Graduate scholarship from the NSERC and the scholarship for graduate programs from the FRQNT.

My favorite hobbies are water activities like swimming, paddleboarding, surfing, and my next water activity that I want to learn is wing foiling. Whenever I can, I go surf in my hometown, Sept-Îles. I also enjoy climbing, especially bouldering.
